Welcome to Adulting 101!

Growing up isn’t easy — and no one gives you an instruction manual. That’s where we come in. Adulthood Ready is designed for teens and young adults who want real advice, practical tools, and honest answers as they navigate life’s next chapter.
From opening a bank account to managing your time, preparing for your first job interview, or simply learning to cook your own meals — we’ve got your back.
Whether you're just getting started or want to level up your life skills, you’re in the right place.

📝Adulthood Readiness Checklist


Check off what you've learned so far — and see how ready you really are!


💰Financial Basics

  • I can create and follow a monthly budget.
  • I understand how debit and credit cards work.
  • I know how to open a bank account.
  • I know how to save money and avoid unnecessary spending.
  • I understand the basics of credit scores and loans.
  • I know how to read a paycheck and understand deductions.

🧠Personal Responsibility

  • I know how to create a daily routine.
  • I manage my own schedule.
  • I keep my living space clean and organized.
  • I do my own laundry regularly.
  • I can identify my personal goals and work toward them.
  • I understand time management and prioritize tasks.

🏥Health & Wellness

  • I can cook at least 3 healthy meals.
  • I’ve booked my own doctor appointment.
  • I know basic first aid and what to do in emergencies.
  • I understand the importance of sleep, hydration, and exercise.
  • I have access to my medical records and know my health history.
  • I know when and how to seek mental health support.

🏢Work & Career Readiness

  • I have a basic resume and know how to update it.
  • I know how to write a professional email or cover letter.
  • I understand how to prepare for a job interview.
  • I know how to search and apply for jobs online.
  • I understand basic workplace etiquette and behavior.
  • I know my basic employee rights and responsibilities.

🛋️Daily Living Skills

  • I know how to grocery shop on a budget.
  • I understand how to use public transportation.
  • I know how to schedule and keep appointments.
  • I can perform basic home maintenance (e.g., plunging a toilet).
  • I can read and follow instructions or manuals.
  • I know how to safely use kitchen appliances.

You are 0% ready to be an adult
